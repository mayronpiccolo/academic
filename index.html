About

I am a clinical and health psychologist studying how people perceive, measure, and respond to mental-health-relevant experiences — from body- and portion-size perception in eating disorders, to the psychometrics of widely used screening instruments, to music-based interventions that support emotional wellbeing and social connection.

My work bridges Harvard University, Cambridge Health Alliance, and Harvard Medical School, with ongoing international collaborations in Switzerland (University of Fribourg, University of Zurich) and Brazil. I work in English and Portuguese, and I am particularly interested in measurement validity across languages and cultures.

Research

Areas of focus

🍽️

Eating-disorder psychopathology

Portion-size perception, perceptual bias, and the Estimation–Experience Index (EEI) methodology in anorexia and bulimia nervosa.

📐

Mental-health measurement & psychometrics

Item response theory, differential item functioning, and cross-linguistic validity of screening instruments such as the PHQ-9 and GAD-7.

🎵

Music-based interventions

Communal singing and music for affect, belonging, and social connectedness, including randomized trials in young children.

🧩

Child & community behavioral health

Preschool behavioral health, prevention, and the social determinants of emotional wellbeing.
